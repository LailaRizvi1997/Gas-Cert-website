Top 5 Gas Safety Tips Every Homeowner Should Know

April 20, 2025 Khawer Rizvi

Ensuring gas safety at home is vital. Faulty gas appliances can lead to serious dangers, including fires, explosions, and carbon monoxide poisoning. Here are five essential tips for every UK homeowner:

1. Schedule Regular Gas Safety Checks

Have your gas appliances, flues, and pipework inspected annually by a Gas Safe registered engineer to maintain safety and efficiency. Our annual gas safety check service provides comprehensive inspections for your peace of mind.

2. Install Carbon Monoxide Alarms

Carbon monoxide is odorless and invisible. Install alarms near gas appliances and sleeping areas, and regularly test them to ensure they're functional.

3. Use Gas Appliances Correctly

Avoid using gas cookers or ovens to heat your home, as misuse can increase risks of carbon monoxide poisoning and fires.

4. Watch for Warning Signs

Recognize signs of faulty appliances, such as yellow or orange flames, soot buildup, and excessive condensation. If these appear, contact a professional immediately.

5. Know Emergency Procedures

In case of a gas leak, turn off your gas supply, open windows and doors, evacuate immediately, and call the National Gas Emergency Service. For urgent response in Walton-on-Thames, our emergency gas call-out service is available 24/7.

By following these simple but crucial safety tips, you can protect your family and property from gas-related hazards. For landlords, these tips are especially important to ensure tenant safety and legal compliance with CP12 certificate requirements.
