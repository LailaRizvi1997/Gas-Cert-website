Landlord's Guide to CP12 Gas Safety Certificates

February 10, 2025 Khawer Rizvi

UK landlords must ensure their rental properties are safe for tenants, primarily through obtaining a CP12 Gas Safety Certificate.

What is a CP12 Certificate?

A CP12 certifies that gas appliances, fittings, and flues have been inspected by a Gas Safe registered engineer and are safe to use.

Legal Requirements for Landlords

  • Conduct annual gas safety checks.
  • Provide tenants with a CP12 copy within 28 days of inspection.
  • Retain records of inspections for at least two years.

Failing to comply can lead to severe penalties and fines.

The CP12 Inspection Checklist

A certified engineer will:

  • Inspect appliances for gas leaks and proper operation.
  • Check ventilation.
  • Test flue flow for safe emission of gases.
  • Confirm safety devices function correctly.

Tips for Landlords

  • Schedule checks 10-12 months after the last inspection to remain compliant.
  • Always employ Gas Safe registered engineers.
  • Provide tenants with gas safety education and CP12 certificates promptly.

As a landlord, ensuring gas safety in your properties is not just a legal requirement but a moral responsibility. Regular CP12 certificates protect your tenants, your property, and your investment.
